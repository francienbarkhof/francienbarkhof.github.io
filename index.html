About Me

I am a graduate of the MSc Artificial Intelligence program at the University of Amsterdam. My research interests are in the field of natural language processing, computer vision, and quantum computing.

I recently completed my thesis, where I worked on neural quantum state tomography, exploring how Transformers and State Space Models can be used to reconstruct quantum states from measurement data (classical shadows).

Right now, I am working as an AI Engineer at WSK Medical, focusing on computer vision projects in the medical domain.

Projects

Structured Neural Shadow Quantum State Tomography

Francien Barkhof
Supervisor: E. Egorov, Examiner: Dr. E. Gavves

Master's thesis, University of Amsterdam, 2026

Links: Code

This work focused on neural quantum state tomography, where machine learning models are used to reconstruct quantum states from measurement data. We researched how the structure of the quantum state can be incorporated into the architecture of neural models representing the quantum state, and into the measurement setting, to improve data efficiency.

Reproducing NevIR: Negation in neural information retrieval

Coen van den Elsen*, Francien Barkhof*, Thijmen Nijdam*, Simon Lupart, Mohammad Aliannejadi

Proceedings of the 48th International ACM SIGIR Conference on Research and Development in Information Retrieval, 2025

Links: Code | Paper | Poster

In this paper, we reproduced and extended the findings of "NevIR: Negation in Neural Information Retrieval". We evaluated the performance of SotA neural IR models with the NevIR benchmark to assess their ability to handle negation, and finetuned IR models on another negation dataset to evaluate how well this understanding of negation generalizes.

Deep learning based tumor detection and segmentation for automated 3D breast ultrasound imaging

Francien Barkhof, Silvia Abbring, Rohit Pardasani, Navchetan Awasthi

IEEE South Asian Ultrasonics Symposium (SAUS), 2024

Links: Paper

This work focused on automating the detection and segmentation of breast tumors in ABUS images. To this end, we trained U-Net and YOLO models, along with pre-processing and post-processing steps, to improve performance.
